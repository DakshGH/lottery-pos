# Pricing strategy — Lottery POS

## What competitors charge (B2B lottery/c-store software)

| Product | Positioning | Pricing signal |
|---|---|---|
| FasTrax Lottery, PDI, ComCash | Full c-store POS suites w/ lottery module | Custom quote; bundled with POS hardware/contracts |
| LottoReco | Dedicated lottery reconciliation, multi-store, white-label | Custom quote, **15-day free trial**, "no hidden fees" |
| Lottostack | Lottery inventory + analytics | Custom quote |
| Market range (per industry roundups) | Dedicated lottery management | **~$50–150 per store / month**, often + setup fee |
| Consumer odds apps (ScratchOdds, ScratcheRater) | Player-facing, *not* retailer | Free or $0.99; irrelevant to us |

**Takeaways**
- Real money is **B2B, per-store, per-month**, quoted privately (so buyers can't comparison-shop easily).
- The big suites are **expensive and complex**. Our edge is **simple + single-purpose + cheap to run** (static web app, near-zero marginal cost). We can sit *just under* the suites and still have ~90%+ margin.
- **Free trial (14–15 days)** is the industry norm — keep it.

Sources: [CStorePOS — lottery management options](https://www.cstorepos.com/blog/lottery-management-software), [LottoReco](https://lottoreco.com/), [GetApp — c-store software w/ lottery](https://www.getapp.com/retail-consumer-services-software/convenience-store/f/lottery-management/), [Gitnux — lottery inventory software](https://gitnux.org/best/lottery-inventory-software/).

## Recommended pricing (per store)

Two plans, as you asked — billed upfront, with the **annual plan the better value** (industry standard; it maximizes cash upfront and cuts churn):

| Plan | Price | Effective / mo | vs. monthly anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| (anchor) Monthly | $59/mo | $59 | — |
| **6 months** | **$294** (one payment) | **$49** | ~17% off |
| **Yearly** | **$468** (one payment) | **$39** | ~34% off — *best value* |

Round, simpler alternative if you prefer: **6-month $300 / Yearly $480**.

> Note on your wording ("yearly a bit less discount than 6 months"): standard practice is the *opposite* — annual gets the **biggest** discount, because you collect a full year upfront and lock in retention. The table above follows that. If you truly want the 6-month plan to be the cheaper-per-month one, just swap the two effective rates — the code/license server doesn't care, it's only a price label.

**Multi-store discounts** (this is where "heavy money" comes from — chains):
- 2–4 stores: 10% off
- 5–9 stores: 20% off
- 10+ stores: custom (still very profitable)

**Add-ons / upsells**
- One-time onboarding/setup: $99 (waive it to close early deals).
- Premium: cloud sync across registers, multi-clerk PINs, priced as a higher tier later.

## Why this makes strong margins
- Hosting a static app + a tiny license server ≈ **$0–25/month total**, regardless of customer count (free tiers cover the start).
- Example: **20 stores on the yearly plan = $9,360/yr**, almost entirely margin. 50 stores ≈ $23k/yr. The cost to serve barely moves.

## Go-to-market (to actually win)
1. **Land your first 3–5 NJ stores free or half-price** in exchange for testimonials + a case study ("cut my nightly count from 30 min to 5").
2. Then publish the prices above and sell on **time saved + theft/shrinkage caught**, not features.
3. **Bill annually upfront** wherever possible (cash flow + retention).
4. Expand store-by-store within a chain; chains are the high-value accounts.
5. Once you have ~10 paying stores, raise prices for new customers (grandfather the early ones).
